Motivation Engine

How Job Design Fuels Engagement, Performance & Satisfaction

The Job Characteristics Model shows that well-designed jobs don’t just fill roles — they ignite motivation. By optimizing five core dimensions, leaders create the psychological conditions for peak performance.

Core Job Dimensions

Skill Variety

Using diverse skills and talents increases engagement and reduces monotony.

Task Identity

Completing a whole, identifiable piece of work fosters ownership and pride.

Task Significance

Believing your work impacts others creates a deeper sense of purpose.

Autonomy

Freedom in how work is done increases responsibility and innovation.

Feedback

Clear, direct feedback enables learning and self-improvement.

⬇️

Psychological States

Experienced Meaningfulness

"This work matters." Driven by skill variety, identity, and significance.

Experienced Responsibility

"I own this." Fueled by autonomy.

Knowledge of Results

"I know how I’m doing." Enabled by direct feedback.

⬇️

Work Outcomes

High Motivation

Employees are energized and committed to their work.

Strong Performance

Quality, efficiency, and innovation improve.

Job Satisfaction

Employees feel fulfilled and less likely to leave.

Leadership Implication

Leaders don’t just assign tasks — they design jobs. To unlock motivation:

  • Combine tasks to increase task identity (e.g., own a project, not just a step).
  • Rotate roles or cross-train to boost skill variety.
  • Connect work to real users/clients to amplify task significance.
  • Delegate decision-making, not just execution, to grow autonomy.
  • Implement fast, clear feedback loops — both formal and informal.

Great jobs create great performance — by design.
