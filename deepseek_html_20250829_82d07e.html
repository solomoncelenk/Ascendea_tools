ADKAR Change Management Model

A structured approach to facilitating change at individual and organizational levels

Awareness
Understanding why change is necessary
Desire
Personal motivation to support and participate in change
Knowledge
Understanding how to change and what to do
Ability
Implementing new skills and behaviors
Reinforcement
Sustaining the change long-term

About the ADKAR Model

The ADKAR model is a goal-oriented change management framework developed by Jeff Hiatt. It guides individual and organizational change through five key elements: Awareness, Desire, Knowledge, Ability, and Reinforcement.

This model structures communication to move stakeholders from initial awareness of a change to full adoption and reinforcement. It's particularly effective for supporting behavioral change and building buy-in during organizational transformations.

Each element represents a milestone that individuals must achieve for change to be successful and lasting.

Communication Planning

ADKAR helps structure messages to address what people need to know at each stage of the change process, ensuring effective communication throughout the transformation.

Change Resistance Management

By identifying where individuals are stuck in the change process, leaders can provide targeted support to move them forward to the next stage.

Training & Development

The model helps design training programs that address knowledge and ability gaps, ensuring employees have the skills needed to implement change.
