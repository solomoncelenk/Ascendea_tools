What's inside

Built from what BMONEY clients learn

Each section covers a real decision point — the kind that determines whether you buy the right property in the right market, or spend 12 months trying to recover from the wrong one.

01
How to Read a Market Before It Moves
The supply and demand indicators that signal a market shift 6–12 months out — stock on market, inventory levels, days on market trends, and vacancy rates. What the headline median price doesn't tell you, and what does.
Market timing
02
LGA vs Suburb vs Pocket — Why the Level Matters
LGA data is a starting point, not a decision. Within every LGA we target, specific pockets consistently outperform the broader median. Understanding which level of data to act on is what separates informed investors from ones who overpay for average stock in a good postcode.
Data interpretation
03
Holding Threshold vs Borrowing Capacity
Your broker gives you a borrowing capacity number. Your real holding threshold — what you can comfortably service when rates move, vacancy hits, or costs shift — is a different calculation entirely. This is the number that determines whether your investment is a long-term asset or a forced sale.
Finance position
04
Due Diligence Beyond the Listing
Zoning changes, approved DAs on neighbouring sites, public housing concentration, infrastructure timelines, and strata fund health — assessable before you inspect, not after you're emotionally committed. The risks that cost investors most are almost never visible from REA or Domain.
Risk identification
05
The Comparable Pricing Method
How to verify what a property is worth using confirmed comparable sales matched by property type, land size, and configuration — before you make an offer, not after. The difference between agent-supplied comparables and independently verified ones can be $40K–$120K on a single transaction.
Pricing confidence
