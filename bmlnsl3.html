How it works

The five-stage process behind every purchase

This isn't improvisation. It's a repeatable framework applied to every client, every property, every decision.

1
Stage 01
Position Mapping
Your budget, risk tolerance, timeline, and non-negotiables are documented before we look at a single property. This filters out 70% of the market immediately — by design.
2
Stage 02
Market Scan + Off-Market Activation
We search both public listings and our agent network simultaneously. Properties are scored against your framework before you see them — you only inspect shortlisted options.
3
Stage 03
Due Diligence & Risk Audit
Council DA checks, strata review, comparable sales analysis, owner-occupier ratios, supply risk assessment. Every shortlisted property runs through the same framework before you consider making a move.
4
Stage 04
Strategy & Negotiation
For auctions: pre-set ceiling, bidding strategy, competitor analysis. For private treaty: market positioning, vendor motivation assessment, structured offers. The strategy matches the method.
5
Stage 05
Acquisition & Settlement
Contract review coordination, building and pest inspection management, finance confirmation. We manage the process through to handover so nothing falls through the cracks at the final stage.

Due diligence discipline

What gets checked on every property

The same framework, applied consistently. This is what separates a considered purchase from an expensive assumption.

01
Council DA applications
Current and approved developments within 500m. Supply risk assessment for the next 3–5 years.
02
Strata & body corporate
Financial health, sinking fund adequacy, outstanding levies, litigation history, planned works.
03
Owner-occupier ratios
ABS data for the specific street and pocket. Investor-heavy markets carry different risk profiles.
04
Comparable sales analysis
Not just suburb-level medians. Street-by-street, property-type-specific pricing analysis.
05
Holding cost stress test
Modelling vacancy, rate increases, and expense changes. Does the property survive a 6-week vacancy at 2% higher rates?
06
Micro-location audit
Arterial road proximity, aspect, flood mapping, heritage overlays, and noise assessment. The things you don't see on Saturday morning.
