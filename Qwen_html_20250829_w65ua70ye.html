The Four Building Blocks of Initiative Leadership

Track, align, and lead with clarity and legitimacy

1. Initiative Charter Design
  • Purpose: Translate vision into a shared agreement.
  • Focus: Define scope, roles, decision rights, and success metrics at the start.
  • Value: Prevents drift by making expectations explicit and shared.
  • Legitimacy Link: Justice — fair distribution of responsibility and transparent ownership
Completed
2. Milestone Discipline
  • Purpose: Break initiatives into steady, credible steps.
  • Focus: Define 3–5 measurable milestones with clear owners and timelines.
  • Value: Creates a rhythm of progress that stakeholders can trust.
  • Legitimacy Link: Performance — consistency in delivery builds credibility
In Progress
3. Sequencing & Prioritization
  • Purpose: Order work logically to prevent resource clashes and wasted effort.
  • Focus: Map dependencies, identify critical paths, apply prioritization logic.
  • Value: Ensures resources are allocated where they matter most.
  • Legitimacy Link: Resonance — progress feels coherent, not chaotic, because priorities are clear
Planned
4. Accountability Assignments
  • Purpose: Make responsibility visible and enforceable.
  • Focus: Apply RACI/DACI models to every milestone and deliverable.
  • Value: Prevents diffusion of responsibility and guarantees follow-through.
  • Legitimacy Link: Justice — fairness in workload + Performance — reliability in outcomes
