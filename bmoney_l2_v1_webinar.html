Why most investors struggle

The problem isn't
lack of information.

There's more property content available today than at any point in Australian history. Most investors who get it wrong weren't under-informed. They were looking at the wrong things ‚Äî or had no way to tell the difference.

Problem 01
Opinion is everywhere. Methodology is almost nowhere.
Every buyer's agent, mortgage broker, and YouTube channel will tell you what to buy. Almost none will show you how they actually made the decision ‚Äî the data pulled, the flag identified, the option rejected and why.
Problem 02
LGA data tells you where to start, not where to buy.
Maitland grew 15.2% in 2025. Ipswich units delivered 103% over 5 years. But within every LGA, specific pockets consistently outperform ‚Äî and others consistently underperform. The headline number is the starting point, not the decision.
Problem 03
The risks that cost investors most aren't in any listing.
Approved DAs on neighbouring sites, strata fund shortfalls, zoning changes, public housing concentration trends ‚Äî all assessable before you inspect. Most buyers find these after they're emotionally committed, or not at all.
Problem 04
Borrowing capacity and holding threshold are two different numbers.
Your broker gives you one. Your real ability to hold through a rate shift, vacancy period, or unexpected cost is the other. On a $700K property, these can differ by $400‚Äì$800 per month. Knowing this before you commit is what separates investors from forced sellers.
